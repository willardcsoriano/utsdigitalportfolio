My Digital Self

Curating our online presence.

Our ‘digital self’ highlights the profound impact social media can have on shaping our identities and social behaviors. It discusses how these platforms often serve as arenas for performing identities, which can sometimes lead to the spread of misinformation and the showcasing of inauthentic selves. This resonates with my observation of younger users who frequently post content that may not necessarily reflect mature or informed viewpoints. This behavior, I believe, not only poses individual risks but also contributes to broader societal issues, such as the degradation of public discourse and the spread of false information.

Currently, I only use the social media platform Facebook, and my interest in social media has waned significantly since my high school days. This shift partly stems from a growing awareness of the potential risks associated with sharing personal information publicly. I believe that indiscriminate sharing can compromise one's safety, reflecting the discussions in the course material about the need for careful self-presentation in digital spaces.

Moreover, discussions regarding this topic suggests that the digital self is a key determinant of our overall identity, heavily influenced by the interactions and feedback we receive in these virtual environments. This complexity underscores my belief in the necessity of age restrictions for social media usage. Such measures could help mitigate the exposure of younger individuals to the potential harms of social media, while also fostering a more responsible and informed user base.

In line with the educational insights, I advocate for more stringent regulations and an increased focus on digital literacies. Educating users about the implications of their online activities could enhance their understanding of the socio-digital landscape, leading to more thoughtful engagement with social media platforms. This approach not only protects individuals but also contributes to the cultivation of a more progressive and informed society.
